About

I'm Daniel Siegle, an AI/ML Engineer based in North Carolina, available for contract work through Possible Futures, LLC. My background spans computational biology, bioinformatics, and machine learning engineering.

I hold an M.S. in Computational Biology from North Carolina Central University (Dept. of Pharmaceutical Sciences), where my thesis — Cytochrome P450 Inhibitor Classification with Statistical Learning — compared Bayesian binary QSAR with scikit-learn models on HTS luminescence assay data across five CYP isozymes (1A2, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, 3A4).

Previously I worked as a Bioinformatics Software Engineer at Q2 Solutions / Q2 Genomics, supporting a satellite NGS lab in Beijing with sequencing analysis, QC, and data transfer pipelines. I've also worked as an ML Engineer, Scientific Programmer, and most recently as an Instructor in AI for Health and Life Sciences at the North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics (NCSSM).

I founded Deep Learning RTP, a 1,700+ member community meetup in the Research Triangle area focused on deep learning, now with over 400 past events.
